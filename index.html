About Us

Founded in 2025, Wambui EK Law Advocates is a progressive and modern law firm located in Nairobi. We are committed to delivering top-tier legal services across a broad spectrum of practice areas. Our core focus lies in public policy, human rights—particularly social, economic, and cultural rights— including food and health, environmental and climate justice, property and land rights, women and child rights, succession and estate planning, conveyancing, and corporate law. Founded by Dr Elizabeth Wambui Kimani-Murage, a distinguished expert with extensive experience in justice promotion, public health policy, food systems, environment, and climate change, our team comprises highly motivated associates with specialized expertise. The Firm is run by our Advocate Barnabas Tanui, with experience in public and private law. Together, we are dedicated to maintaining the highest standards of legal practice, enhancing societal impact, and transformative change in the justice experience for society.

Meet the team.

Dr. Elizabeth Wambui Kimani-Murage

Elizabeth Wambui Kimani is the Proprietor at Wambui EK Law Advocates. She holds a Bachelor of Laws from the University of Nairobi, and a Postgraduate Diploma from the Kenya School of Law, alongside a Bachelor’s of Environmental Health and a Masters of Public Health from Moi University, and a PhD in Public Health from the University of the Witwatersrand, South Africa.

She has longstanding experience in environmental health, public health, social justice and human rights issues. She also has longstanding leadership experience.

As the Proprietor, she provides oversight to ensure the Firm’s vision and values are upheld, the mission is accomplished, standards are maintained, and clients are satisfied.

Barnabas Tanui

Mr Barnabas Tanui is an Associate Advocate at Wambui EK Law Advocates with a practical and reasonable approach to the legal practice. Barnabas holds a Bachelor of Laws degree (LLB) from Africa Nazarene University and a Postgraduate Diploma from the Kenya School of Law.

He will be in charge of the daily running of the Firm.

He brings on board good experience from both public and private sectors. He previously served at the State Law office alongside state counsels in civil litigation matters defending the Government. In the private sector, he has handled matters in diverse areas including succession and estate planning, conveyancing, debt collection and insurance claims. His previous training has been focused around litigation and dispute resolution where he has a keen interest and determination on professional growth and development.

His previous leadership roles have refined his organizational, communication and people skills which are an added asset to the practice.

Core Practice Areas

  • Human Rights

    Economic, social, and cultural rights, including health, education, and food systems justice.

  • Women and Child Rights

    Gender equality, child protection, property rights, and socio-economic empowerment for women and children.

  • Environmental Rights

    Legal advocacy on nature’s rights, biodiversity protection, and climate justice—including loss and damage compensation.

  • Land & Property Rights

    Protection of land tenure, housing rights, property ownership, and community land governance.

  • Succession & Estate Planning

    Management and disposal of a deceased person’s estate to heirs. Anticipating, during one’s lifetime, such disposal through - creation of wills, trusts, power of attorney, gifts and property ownership/transfer.

  • Corporate Law

    Company formation, governance, regulatory compliance, contracts, and dispute resolution for businesses.

  • Conveyancing

    Facilitation of legal transfer of property ownership, real estate transactions, and land due diligence.

  • Consultancy

    Legal advisory for NGOs, civil society, and government in human rights, environmental law, and governance reforms.
